What We Do

Applied engineering for real-world infrastructure challenges.

Active Innovation Research Labs is an independent applied-engineering research organization focused on developing, testing, and deploying technologies that improve reliability, accessibility, and visibility across critical infrastructure systems.

Our work bridges the gap between theory and practice by combining rigorous system design with continuous measurement, field validation, and practical deployment.

Current Research

Solar Mesh is our first major research program.

Solar Mesh

Solar Mesh is a low-cost monitoring and communication platform for small-scale off-grid solar systems. The research focuses on improving visibility into panel input, battery status, load output, and overall system health.

Problem

Many small solar systems fail silently, leaving users and technicians without clear information about performance, battery health, or failure conditions.

Solution

A field-deployable monitoring platform that measures power data, communicates system status, and supports remote diagnostics.

Impact

Better reliability, improved maintenance decisions, longer system life, and greater trust in distributed energy systems.

Why This Matters

Reliable infrastructure requires visibility.

Many infrastructure systems operate with little visibility into their health, performance, or failure conditions. This is especially true in constrained, remote, and underserved environments where maintenance resources are limited and system failures can have serious consequences.

Our research focuses on improving reliability through better measurement, diagnostics, communication, and system design.
